Ennui

Ennui covers me
A drab vitality that won’t suffice
I don’t remember
When my mind was dancing
Into the sunset
Seems so long ago now
A big mistake
Carbon copied again and again
It’s raining inside
And when the ship crashed
Much damage
Wrenching from all continuum
Look, the time
Days fall like dominoes
Yet I remember
What a sunny day feels like
Sweet warmth
A whispering of cool breeze
It’s fiction now
Perhaps a tale told by a fool
Yet I wonder
If I could scroll back in time
Feel there
Rewind the tape and find the groove
Stay there
Is it really just a photograph?
Look, the time
Time pounced, shipwrecked
A thief in the night
A twister beneath my feet, I vanish
Into the clouds
My wings, broken, beaten, and bound
My feet, sore
And I wonder if I can take it anymore
Again and again
Drift off and wake, uncomfortable
Still confused
I’m tied up, I can hear the train
Still raining
Drab will never be enough.
