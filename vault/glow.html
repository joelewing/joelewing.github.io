Glow

One day, while out in the snow,

beneath the white flakes, I saw a faint glow.

I drew nearer to see the light,

a radiance that glowed a cold yellow-white.

As I picked it up in my hand,

I felt as if the universe was whispering an unspoken demand.

I took it inside, against my gut,

Because I wished to discover its contents, containing what.

I pondered, but decided not to show it to Mom,

who knows, she might fear it a bomb.

I left the object by my bedside that night,

but woke up a few hours later in fright.

A little man stood there, the strange object his source,

He spoke my terrestrial English but his voice a little hoarse.

Though I was big compared to his fairy small,

Through his magic, I was reduced to the size of his ball.

There it was, a spaceship, shining bright

I was now clearly seeing what I had found earlier that night.

Inviting me, to with him fly away,

I was at first nervous, but then said okay.

He took me on a marvelous flight,

to see the wonders of the universe that night.

And buried beneath the nebular snow,

there was the sun of my solar system, a faint glow.
